Baiting



involves tricking users into downloading and installing malware onto their system. For example a threat actor leaves USB keys loaded with auto installing malware and leaves them in public places or in a more targeted attack in a company’s office, they do this in hopes that an unsuspecting victim plugs this USB key into their own or somebody else’s device out of curiosity and then the malware is installed usually ransomware of some description.

Another example of this is when hackers will send emails to victims with a link to a seemingly legitimate website that actually installs malware on their system or on some actual legitimate website there may be ads that lead to websites that automatically download malware such as adware, spyware or in the worst-case scenario ransomware.
